Hosting Your Website

Make your Website go Live!

Welcome to this bonus lesson on hosting your website.

What exactly is Web Hosting?: It's basically the process of making your websites available anywhere on the internet.

How do we do that?: Well, in order for it to be available to everyone, first we have to put our website & all of the files & data that it consists of, so our index.html, our images, etc, all onto a web server.

What's different about a web server is that it's actually connected to the internet. So what that means is that the stuff that you put on there will be available 24/7 & ready for anyone from anywhere in the world to access all of these files. And that's what's required to be able to view your website.

Now @ the moment you're able to view your website locally coz all of the files are on your computer. Right? And this is what we call local development. It means that all of the files & folders are locally available on your computer.

It's this process of taking all of the files & folders that make up your website & putting that onto a web server that's hooked up to the internet, this process is what we call web hosting.

https://github.com/
  1. Now you'll only see the private option if you actually pay for a GitHub account, but we want to be able to host our website for everyone to see, so we want to make sure that public is the one we select.
  2. Then go ahead & tick the box & add a README file. This is very important, otherwise it might be quite difficult for us to upload our files.
  3. Then finally go ahead & click "Create Repository".

It's really important that your home page code is inside index.html coz this is what GitHub is going to look to in order to render your homepage. It's a good idea to also just check the spelling & make sure it's a lowercase "i" & its index.html.

Now, again of note, it's important that you don't drag the entire folder in, that's not going to work, you want to drag in the contents of that folder.

Now, once that's done, you can see it has uploaded all of those files & now you can click on "Commit changes" to add those files to GitHub.

Once your files have processed, which can take several minutes & you might need to refresh the page a few times in order to get the page up & running, then make sure that all of your folders & files are definitely listed, if they're not, then you might have to go ahead & upload them again.

Once you've verified that, then go into Settings, & inside Settings navigate to Pages & then inside Pages we're going to change the part under Branch. So we're going to change from none to main branch which contains our root folder & all of the files that we just uploaded. So go ahead & hit Save for that to go through.

Once that's done, you should be able to refresh the page a couple of times & you should see this little pop up which says that your site is live @ this location.

Now, just another heads up, this process can take anywhere between 1 to 10 minutes. Depending on how busy GitHub is, how many people are using it, this process can take a while.

Now, this is an important point, sometimes you might get this error message, simply just go back & try again.

Once you've refreshed this particular page & you see this little box here, then that means we're ready to go ahead & visit our website. So click on Visit site, & you can check to see all the pages work & make sure that's exactly how you had it locally.

Once you're done, you can take this url & share it with anybody across the world & they will be able to see your portfolio.
